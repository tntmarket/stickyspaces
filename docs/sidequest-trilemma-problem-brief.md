# Problem Brief: The Side-Quest Trilemma

**Date**: 2026-02-20

---

## Who

Software developers and knowledge workers with ADHD or limited working memory who juggle multiple interrelated tasks on macOS. They have strong spatial memory — remembering things well when keyed by location or structure — but struggle to hold abstract task relationships in their head.

---

## The Trilemma

When a valuable side-quest appears during focused work, there are only bad options:

1. **Explore and lose track** — Dive into the side-quest, enter a hyperfocus trance, and hours later realize the original goal is forgotten entirely.
2. **Explore while anchoring** — Try to hold the original goal in working memory while exploring. Most mental effort goes to "refreshing memory" rather than making progress, resulting in circular thinking and exhaustion.
3. **Refuse and suffer** — Stay on the main task under sub-optimal conditions, frustrated that a valuable improvement must be deferred because of scope creep risk.

None of these outcomes are acceptable. A fourth option is needed: **explore freely, retreat safely, never lose your place.**

---

## Underlying Needs

### 1. Branch off without disrupting

When I spot a valuable side-quest, I need to pursue it immediately without destroying the context of what I'm currently working on. The switch must be instant and zero-effort — any friction and I'll either skip the side-quest or forget to preserve my current state.

### 2. Orient — "What am I doing here?"

After surfacing from a hyperfocus trance or being interrupted, I need to instantly recall what I was doing and how it relates to my bigger goals. Re-orienting should be a glance, not an exhausting effort to reconstruct my mental state.

### 3. Navigate — "Let me get back to…"

When juggling several tasks and side-quests, I need to move between related tasks fluidly. I should never have to remember where to go in order to resume a task.

### 4. Clean up — "I'm done with this"

When a side-quest is finished, it should fade away and return me to where I branched off. Completed work shouldn't clutter my view, and I should naturally resume the parent task without thinking about it.

---

## Why Current Tools Fail

macOS Mission Control partially addresses this by mapping one task per workspace, but:

- **No relationships**: Workspaces are a flat, one-dimensional row. There's no way to see that workspace 5 is a side-quest spawned from workspace 2.
- **No orientation**: Tiny thumbnails don't convey what a workspace is for or how deep you are in a side-quest chain.
- **No memory aid**: You must remember which numbered workspace corresponds to which task — exactly the kind of mental bookkeeping that exhausts limited working memory.

---

## What Success Looks Like

- **Task capacity**: Sustaining progress on noticeably more concurrent tasks/side-quests than today's 2-3 workspace limit before cognitive overload.
- **Focus depth**: Side-quest exploration feels "free" rather than anxious — no background hum of "don't forget the original task."
- **Context-switch quality**: Returning to a parent task feels like picking up exactly where you left off.

**Validation**: Self-reported assessment after 2 weeks of daily use. The user will know if it's working.
